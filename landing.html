THE HARDWARE

Two buttons. That's the whole interface.

Built for a parent in distress, not a power user. A tiny display, a Select button to pick the child, and a Trigger button to raise the alarm. No menus, no screens to read.

▸
Tap to cycle
Select steps through each child by index — 1, 2, 3 — on the on-band display.
▸
Hold to broadcast
A long press fires a missing-child packet for that child straight to the network.
▸
Days of battery
Low-power radio and a featherweight payload mean a single charge lasts the whole event.
BUILT FOR THE WORST DAY

Every decision optimised for chaos.

Internet-optional by design

LoRa first, raw SMS as fallback. Nothing depends on the mobile data that always dies in a crowd.

Landmark geofencing

Coordinates resolve to names people actually use — "near Gate 7", "by Car Park 3" — not raw lat/long.

Unlimited children per family

Add as many bands as a parent needs. Each gets its own index, mapped to a physical wristband.
